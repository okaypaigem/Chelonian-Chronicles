Tortoises

Also known as Testudinidae

image of a tortoise, taken by Susanne Edele

What even is a Tortoise?

An animal with a thick, hard shell that it can move its head and legs into for protection. It eats plants, moves very slowly, and sleeps during the winter. (https://dictionary.cambridge.org/us/dictionary/english/tortoise)"

Information

The table below is showing information about species of Tortoises, their environments, and unique traits they have.

Tortoise Species Environment Unique Traits
Sonoran Desert Tortoise Arid desert, rocky hillsides Builds burrows, stores water in its bladder
Galápagos Tortoise Volcanic islands, grasslands Largest tortoise, can live over 100 years
Sulcata Tortoise Dry savannahs of Africa Digs extensive burrows to escape extreme heat
Indian Star Tortoise Dry scrub forests, grasslands in India Star-patterned shell for camouflage
Leopard Tortoise Semi-arid regions in Africa Domed shell with spotted markings
Aldabra Giant Tortoise Coastal grasslands, swamps in Seychelles Extremely large, grazes on grasses and low plants
